the longer story

Early in my career, my primary focus was trying to figure out how to better engage people in classical music, a field that has struggled in recent decades to evolve with the changing needs of audiences. As a sandbox to explore new ways to innovate concert experiences, I co-founded an ensemble called Conduit, where I also played clarinet from 2017-2020. In 2018, I pitched an ensemble residency to Knight Foundation and secured $180,000 grant for Conduit to be housed at University of Miami full-time for 2 years. In this residency, we pioneered a service design framework for the arts called Audience Experience Design (AXD), which I continue to develop with Siggi Bachmann at New World Symphony in Miami Beach. We focused on building AXD through the creation of performing arts experiences for millennial audiences, which resulted in a performance experience called Care. As part of our design work, we also prototyped the very beginning of a mobile app called Ensemble to help make concert-going more social.

Before Conduit, I played three seasons as a Clarinet Fellow at New World Symphony. While playing two or three concerts a week, I designed and produced two live performance experiences, DIMENSIONS and transmuse. DIMENSIONS was an interactive performance geared toward a younger-than-average audience that had no previous experience with classical music. This program empowered audience members to explore many different ways of listening through the orchestra's favorite pieces of music through extramusical creative activity including writing, drawing, and crafting paper models in order to discover their own pathway into classical music. I spoke about this project and the (unexpectedly) evolving nature of classical music in my TEDx talk and at the Classical:NEXT conference in Rotterdam, both in 2017. My final project at New World, transmuse was a musical experience created in collaboration with transgender community in South Florida to celebrate the trans experience, sourcing stories from indviduals who identify as trans and fusing them with music to create a space to bring the community together. I collaborated with Siggi on both projects, and as each had a strong focus on designing the events through the eyes of our audiences, this collaboration led to our creation of Audience Experience Design.
